Setup

1

Download and open

Open the DMG and drag Silver Remote to your Applications folder.

2

Connect to the same network

Make sure your Mac and Apple TV are on the same Wi-Fi network. Silver Remote uses your local network to communicate with your Apple TV.

3

Launch and control

Click the [tv] icon in your menu bar. Silver Remote scans the network every 30 seconds to find your Apple TV. Once connected, the status dot turns green and shows the device IP.

Requirements

FAQ

Does it work with Apple TV 4K or Apple TV HD?

No. Silver Remote is built for the Apple TV 2nd and 3rd generation, which use the DMAP protocol. The 4th generation and later use a different protocol (MRP) that is not currently supported.

How does it find my Apple TV?

Silver Remote scans your local network every 30 seconds using the pyatv library. It discovers any Apple TV automatically. No manual IP configuration is needed.

My Apple TV isn't connected to Wi-Fi yet. Can I still use this?

Your Apple TV needs to be on the same network as your Mac. If it's not yet on Wi-Fi, connect it to your router with an Ethernet cable first, then use Silver Remote to navigate the Wi-Fi settings on screen.

Is it free?

Yes, completely free and open source. You can view the source code on GitHub.

Do I need to install Python or any dependencies?

No. The DMG contains a self-contained app with everything bundled. Just drag it to Applications and run.

How do I quit the app?

Click the [tv] icon in your menu bar to open the remote, then click "Quit" at the bottom.
